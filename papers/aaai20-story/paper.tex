\begin{abstract}

Predicting endings for narrative stories is a grand challenge for
machine commonsense reasoning. The task requires accurate representation of
the story semantics and logic structures. 
Pretrained language models, such as Bert, made progress recently in
this task by exploiting spurious statistical cues in the
test dataset, and not ``understanding'' the stories per se.  
In this paper, we propose to improve the representation
of stories by first simplifying the sentences to some key concepts
and second modeling the latent relationship between the key ideas within the story.
Such enhanced sentence representation, when used with pretrained language models, 
makes subtantial gains in prediction accuracies on the popular ROCStory
Cloze Test without using the biased validation data. 
\end{abstract}
