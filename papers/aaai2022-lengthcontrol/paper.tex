\begin{abstract}
%Due to space constraints in real applications,
%controlling the summary length in abstractive summarization is an important problem. 
Previous length-controllable models that control termination of decoding 
within desired length cannot adapt the information selection for different lengths.
%The lengths of generated summaries are close to the summary length of training set, as the summary lengths in training set are always concentrated in a specific range.
%Moreover, length control is weak in that the lengths of generated summaries
They also tend to generate summaries with similar length of those in the training data.
In this paper,
we propose a {\bf length-aware attention mechanism} (LAAM) to 
adjust the information to be summarized in source document based on length constraints.
We create a {\bf length-balance dataset} (LBD)
consisting of documents from the original data and their extractive summaries 
evenly distributed across different length ranges.
We apply LAAM on BART, train it with LBD as pretrained information selection model 
and fine-tune the original dataset on it.
%In this paper, we propose a noval approach to constrain the summary length, 
%which first creates a pretraining dataset with a proper {\em summary length distribution} from original training dataset and then trains our new length-controllable model based on information selection on created pretraining dataset and original dataset successively.
The results show that our approach is effective in generating
high-quality summaries with desired lengths and 
even those lengths never seen 
in the original training set.
%Our approach outperforms the baselines on CNN/Daily Mail and XSUM.
\end{abstract}
