\begin{abstract}
    Large-scale pre-trained language models~(PLMs) have advanced the state-of-the-art across a wide variety of natural language processing tasks, but their humongous model size often pose challenges to the practial deployment due to memory and latency requirement. Knowledge distillation~(KD) is one of the most popular methods to reduce model size by transferring knowledge from a large teacher model to a smaller student model. Though offering improvements, the rich knowledge of the teacher model may not be effectively transferred due to the limited data from downstream tasks. In this papaer, we propose Learning to Augment~(LAT), a novel data augmentation approach to enhance task-specific knowledge distillation for language model compression. In LAT, a data augmentor learns to generate informative samples such that the student model's generalization error on an unseen meta validation set  is minimized after distillation. Experimental results on diverse language understanding tasks show that LAT enables more effective teacher-student knowledge transfer and produces more accurate student model , especially under low-resource scenario.
\end{abstract}
