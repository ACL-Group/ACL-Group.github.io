\begin{abstract}
Depression is a prominent health challenge to the world, and detecting depression from online posts can be a promising technique for combating the threat. However, existing methods for online depression detection are not practically applicable, due to their lack of generalizability, explainability and efficiency in early detection. To tackle these challenges, we propose a psychiatric scale guided risky post screening method that can capture risky posts related to the dimensions defined in clinical depression scales, and providing interpretable diagnostic basis. A Hierarchical Attentional Network equipped with BERT is proposed to further advance explainable predictions. For early detection, we propose an online algorithm based on an evolving queue of risky posts that can significantly reduce the number of model inferences to boost efficiency. Experiments show that our method outperforms the competitive feature-based and neural models under both in-domain and cross-domain settings. Our method also exhibits superiority in both effectiveness and efficiency in early detection. 
\footnote{We will release our code after acceptance.}
\end{abstract}
