Title ActionNet: An Action Concept Lexicon Automatically Extracted from Text Corpus
Masked Reviewer ID: Assigned_Reviewer_1
Review:
Question 
Overall Rating Reject
Summary of the paper (what is specifically being proposed, and in what context) and a brief justification for your overall recommendation; one paragraph The paper presents a novel approach to extract noun concepts (called action concepts) of objects (or arguments) in verb phrases. The approach allows the same verb to appear with different concepts, together representing a good coverage of possible nouns appearing with that verb. The approach is supervised in that it uses a taxonomy and a derived concept graph.
Three (or more!) strong points about the paper. Please be precise and explicit; clearly explain the value and nature of the contribution. (S1) The discussed problem is interesting and has not received sufficient attention in information extraction.

(S2) The presented approach is easy to follow and clearly explained.

(S3) The evaluation uses a credible data set.
Three (or more!) weaknesses of the paper. Please indicate clearly whether the paper has any mistakes, missing related work, or results that cannot be considered a contribution. Write it so the authors understand what are seen as the negatives. (W1) Unfortunately, the algorithm is low on insights or novelty with a fairly crude reduction to k-clique and a straightforward branch-and-bound implementation. This is reflected in high running times for larger concept spaces.
It is also unfortunate that the two provided confidence functions are just simple trades for recall or precision, respectively. The authors should be encouraged to investigate whether there might be confidence functions better tuned to the specific problem that provide an insightful solution that boosts both recall and precision over these simple baseline functions.

(W2) The evaluation is generally not convincing and fails to substantiate several claims:
(a) "But to date, the scale of ReVerb is far from sufficient to cover all triples that ever appear in text."
"We give up using ReVerb data due to these limitations." These statements need to be substantiated by an actual evaluation. In particular, as it is not clear if ReVerb retrained on the Google corpus might already obtain significantly higher accuracy comparable to what is shown here.
(b) Evaluation: it would have been nice to understand the difference in quality of result, when using WordNet vs. Probase as concept space.
(c) Accuracy for determining verb sense is very low, similarly for word-sense disambiguation. The reported numbers of Senseval-3 are obviously not comparable to the usual use of Senseval-3 as they are using a subset that is not easily compared to the numbers of WSD reported by other systems. Nevertheless, they are significantly lower than what has been reported for the general Senseval-3 case, and the authors fail to comment on this nor include sufficient related work. See [1] for typical numbers reported for Senseval-3.
[1] Khaled Abdalgader and Andrew Skabar. 2012. Unsupervised similarity-based word sense disambiguation using context vectors and sentential word importance. ACM Trans. Speech Lang. Process. 9, 1, Article 2 (May 2012), 21 pages. DOI=10.1145/2168748.2168750 http://doi.acm.org/10.1145/2168748.2168750

(W3) "The generalization processes in these proposals are only based on the relation instances in ReVerb, without using external knowledge, i.e., taxonomies. Therefore, these proposals still suffer from a limited scale." The first part of this sentence does not hold for some of the recent generalizations of ReVerb or ReVerb-like approaches such as [2,3]. Both generalize patterns using taxonomies or similar semantic structures, yet the authors fail to properly address them.

[2] Andrea Moro and Roberto Navigli. 2012. WiSeNet: building a wikipedia-based semantic network with ontologized relations. Question 
Overall Rating Reject
Summary of the paper (what is specifically being proposed, and in what context) and a brief justification for your overall recommendation; one paragraph This paper proposes techniques for abstracting the arguments of verbs in text into “action concepts”. Semantic role labeling requires manual work and does not scale. On the other extreme, fine-grained techniques like ReVerb do not capture all possible verb arguments. Hence, the solution is to automatically create action concepts by finding the maximal concepts of the arguments that minimally overlap with each other. Experiments show how action concepts can help many NLP applications.
Three (or more!) strong points about the paper. Please be precise and explicit; clearly explain the value and nature of the contribution. S1: Storing verbs along with their action concepts is useful for understanding sentences.
S2: The action conceptualization algorithms are simple and effective.
S3: The experiments show various NLP use cases of action concepts.
Three (or more!) weaknesses of the paper. Please indicate clearly whether the paper has any mistakes, missing related work, or results that cannot be considered a contribution. Write it so the authors understand what are seen as the negatives. W1: The experiments are missing many details.
W2: A key citation (Ollie) that extends ReVerb is not cited or evaluated.
W3: This paper is suitable for an NLP conference rather than a database conference like SIGMOD.
Is the paper relevant for SIGMOD? No
Significance The paper improves on existing work
Technical depth and quality of content Solid work
Validation - experiments and proofs Unclear/obscure, hard to determine what is going on and what has been validated
Presentation Reasonable: improvements needed
Discussion of related work - recall that the new page limits allow for material outside the 12pp including references, hence we expect good coverage of related work Clear explanation of the state of the art and how this paper relates
Detailed Evaluation (contribution, pros/cons, errors); please number each point D1. Please add a citation to Ollie (Mausam, M. et al. Open language learning for information extraction. In EMNLP-CoNLL, pages 523–534, 2012.), which significantly improves ReVerb. I would also make a comparison with Ollie instead of ReVerb.
D2. It is not clear if action concepts really solves the bigger problem of understanding sentences. There are many other difficult problems including understanding adjectives, analyzing compound nouns, and paraphrasing. It would be helpful to give a high-level picture of all the challenges and explain why understanding verbs is important.
D3. Page 2: some details are missing. What are the three concepts for “spend”? Also, I do not see the term “habit” in Table 1.
D4. TFIDF definition in Page 4: By # of verbs, do you mean the total number of verbs in all documents?
D5. Lemma 2 in Page 4: The approximation ratio does not look practical. If tau is close to 0, then the second formula becomes 0+2 / 0, which is infinity.
D6. Algorithm 1, Page 5: Please add a running example for the algorithm.
D7. Figure 4, Page 5: Please add values to the Avg. Concept Size and k axes.
D8. Page 6: By only considering verbs with one argument, aren’t you losing a lot of information compared to verbs with both subjects and objects?
D9. The experiments are missing many details.
Section 4.4: Is it easy to compute Precision(v)?
Section 4.7: What is the action frame generation problem? What is the intuition for Prob(c,v)? What is polysemy of verbs? Why do you use the Fuzzy C-means algorithm and how well does it work compared to other methods? How did you come up with the condition for determining if a verb belongs to a cluster?
Section 4.8: Why does the taxonomy-based method not work well for “company” and “stock”? Table 7 needs more explanation on why the combined approach works best.

Summary of the paper (what is specifically being proposed, and in what context) and a brief justification for your overall recommendation; one paragraph In this paper the authors propose to abstract the verb arguments into a set of "action concepts". Although the motivation is of practical interest, and the methods presented seem valid, this kind of work should probably be rather presented to a conference related to NLP (e.g. ACL, NAACL, COLING).
