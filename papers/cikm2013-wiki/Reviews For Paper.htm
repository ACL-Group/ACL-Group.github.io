Title Wikification via Co-occurrence
Masked Reviewer ID: Assigned_Reviewer_1

Question 
Brief summary of the paper and a concise rationale for your recommendation This paper proposes to disambiguate phrase senses by wikipedia-based link co-occurrence statistics. As wikipedia articles are sparsely linked, they first propose an iterative enrichment method to add more links to wikipedia articles. With the enriched links, they are able to "wikify" a plain news text and thus to disambiguate the senses in that text. Experiments are done to evaluate the effectiveness of the iterative enrichment as well as the end-to-end results. However, the paper is incremental in terms of the idea and lacking principle in terms of the technique (See W1 and W2).
Major strengths (explain the nature and value of the paper's contributions) S1. The paper addresses an important and practical problem.

S2. The paper is easy to follow.
Major weaknesses (indicate any limitations of the paper, notably technical errors, missing related work, and recycled results) W1. The idea of using link co-occurrence is incremental (see D1).

W2. The solution is quite straightforward and many decisions seem arbitrary (see D2).

W3. Experimental comparison to the baseline methods is insufficient (see D3).

W4. Related work lacks comparison and contrast (see D4).
Intellectual novelty (give High to papers on new topics or proposing stimulating new ideas; Medium to papers on well known topics, but making useful contributions; Low to incremental papers, or papers with known ideas) Low
Technical depth (assess the depth of the contributions) Low
Presentation quality (assess the quality of the presentation of the contributions?) Acceptable
Detailed comments D1. Using link co-occurrence instead of word co-occurrence is incremental. In fact, the fundamental idea is the same (we can simply treat a link as a "word").

D2. The solution is easy to come up, lacking a novel underlying model or principle which would advance the literature. In addition, many decisions, while plausible, appear arbitrary (eg, threshold tau, sliding window size). No analytical or empirical evidence is presented to convince the reader that these decisions are robust.

D3. Experimental comparison with baselines in Table III simply report figures from previous paper, and thus has some blank cells. The authors should try to implement the baselines so that they can run them on all the datasets for a complete comparison.
Also, in Table I, only the original matrix and the matrix after iterations are compared; how about showing the precision after different numbers of iterations?

D4. Related work section mostly lists existing works with very limited comparing and contrasting with the solution presented in this paper. The authors should highlight their differences.
Reviewer confidence High
Overall recommendation Weak Reject (I tend to favor Reject, but will not argue against acceptance)

Question 
Brief summary of the paper and a concise rationale for your recommendation This paper talks about a very interesting field of research: linking the concepts to Wikipedia articles. The machine linking research can benefit many applications and areas in NLP, specifically WSD. The authors proposed an iterative framework to rather enrich the linking in the existing Wikipedia pages or to link the concepts of a plain text to the corresponding Wikipedia articles. The authors provided interesting results on different set of data using Wikipedia for disambiguation.
Major strengths (explain the nature and value of the paper's contributions) The paper is very clear and the authors tried to visualize their method through many examples which makes the paper more readable. The paper is very well motivated and the experiments and results clearly show the potential of their approach. The authors also conducted some experiments to support the efficiency of their system in dealing with large datasets.
Major weaknesses (indicate any limitations of the paper, notably technical errors, missing related work, and recycled results) 1- There are a few studies in this area that are missing in the paper. I believe the authors should have mentioned them in the related work and also used them as a term of comparison. As examples:
• L. Ratinov and D. Roth and D. Downey and M. Anderson, Local and Global Algorithms for Disambiguation to Wikipedia. ACL (2011)
• Sara Tonelli, Claudio Giuliano, Kateryna Tymoshenko, Wikipedia-based WSD for multilingual frame annotation.
• The WikiMachine: http://thewikimachine.fbk.eu/
• Alchemy API

2- I did not find the framework and approach proposed by the authors very novel in comparison with TF-IDF methods, co-occurrences matrices and vector space models.

3- I’m wondering if the authors have conducted any cleaning and preprocessing over the Wikipedia articles. There is no information regarding the tokenization either. Moreover, I believe that POS tagging could help the chunking phase and why the authors did not try it. There are also some intuitions such as “longer terms are more likely to be accurate” that need some evidences or analysis.

4- The authors should have been clear about how they chose the window size in matrix enrichment phase. I can predict some changes in the performance when window size varies. Moreover, the window size 5 or 10 seems to be a good approximation based on Fig 6 but I could not figure out what was the window size setting in the experiments. Figure 6 in the paper is located after figure 7. It might be a bit odd to look for figure 6 after figure 7.

Question 
Brief summary of the paper and a concise rationale for your recommendation The authors present a novel unsupervised method for Wikification of given text. They argue that Wikification can be seen a generalized version of Word Sense Disambiguation (WSD) problem, and specifically attack WSD for Multi-word Expressions (MWE).

The authors develop a framework to parse and compute co-occurrence matrix of Wikipedia concepts; and an iterative method to enrich this matrix for sparsely linked concepts. They use a sliding window algorithm for disambiguation of given input text using this co-occurrence matrix.

They evaluate their method again two other unsupervised methods using three datasets. They also show various properties of their own approach change with respect to each other in different settings.

Since Wikipedia is dominated by noun concepts, the proposed method works well for nouns and noun phrases only. The experiments are limited to English language. It would be interesting to see how such an approach would work for languages like German (nouns can be combined to form words) or Mandarin (graphical language).

My overall recommendation is Weak Accept.
Major strengths (explain the nature and value of the paper's contributions) The authors have applied good optimization techniques for parsing and merging chunks of noun phrases. They have significantly increased the coverage of the concepts co-occurrence matrix using an iterative algorithm to enrich it. The have handled both unlinked terms and commonly occurring terms in Wikipedia articles. The tunable sliding window algorithm allows for disambiguation of several unlinked terms together within a local context; and then aggregates results from each local context to perform a psuedo-global decision.


The authors have used well known datasets, and their evaluations sound repeatable. Their demo website parsed concepts reasonably for couple of CNN new stories. Their results compare favorably with other well known methods.
Major weaknesses (indicate any limitations of the paper, notably technical errors, missing related work, and recycled results) The basic limitation for this paper is that while it attempts to generalize the problem of WSD as Wikification, it only solves parts of WSD related to noun and noun phrases.

All of the work in focused on English language, and it would have been interesting if the authors had attempted even a base line experiment with another language. I would like to think that Wikipedia coverage for some of the other languages like French, German, etc. might be significantly lower than English, and it would have been instructive to know how that would have affected the effectiveness of the approach.

The authors have not mentioned the expression for the function 'g' from Section III/B that was used in the experimental evaluation.

Detailed comments The authors present a novel unsupervised method for Wikification of given text. They argue that Wikification can be seen a generalized version of Word Sense Disambiguation (WSD) problem, and specifically attack WSD for Multi-word Expressions (MWE).

The authors have applied good optimization techniques for parsing and merging chunks of noun phrases. They have significantly increased the coverage of the concepts co-occurrence matrix using an iterative algorithm to enrich it. The have handled both unlinked terms and commonly occurring terms in Wikipedia articles. The tunable sliding window algorithm allows for disambiguation of several unlinked terms together within a local context; and then aggregates results from each local context to perform a psuedo-global decision.

They evaluate their method again two other unsupervised methods using three datasets. They also show various properties of their own approach change with respect to each other in different settings.

The authors have used well known datasets, and their evaluations sound repeatable. Their demo website parsed concepts reasonably for couple of CNN new stories.

The basic limitation for this paper is that while it attempts to generalize the problem of WSD as Wikification, it only solves parts of WSD related to noun and noun phrases.

All of the work in focused on English language, and it would have been interesting if the authors had attempted even a base line experiment with another language. I would like to think that Wikipedia coverage for some of the other languages like French, German, etc. might be significantly lower than English, and it would have been instructive to know how that would have affected the effectiveness of the approach.

The authors have not mentioned the expression for the function 'g' from Section III/B that was used in the experimental evaluation.

Based on these strengths and weakness of the paper, I recommend "Weak Accept".
