\begin{abstract}
%Capturing cross-cultural differences between terms is an important challenge in bilingual text understanding.

People of distinct cultures often hold different opinions on the same named entities, resulting in cross-cultural differences.
Understanding slang terms across languages requires the knowledge of cross-cultural similarities. 
In this paper, we present a lightweight yet effective framework for mining such cross-cultural differences and similarities from social media, and evaluate it on two newly proposed tasks: 1) mining cross-cultural differences of named entities and 2) finding similar terms for slang across languages. 
The proposed framework can potentially become a building block for cross-cultural/lingual social media analysis.
Extensive experiments show that our framework substantially outperforms a number of baseline methods on both tasks.
\end{abstract}
