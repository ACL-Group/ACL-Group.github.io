Paper# 772: Deep Cascade Multi-task Learning for Slot Filling in Chinese E-commerce Shopping Guide Assistant

Authors
Yu Gong (Contact Author)
Xusheng Luo
Kenny Zhu
Xi Chen
Wenwu Ou
Lu Duan
Abstract

Slot filling is a critical task in natural language understanding (NLU) for dialog systems. State-of-the-art solutions regard it as a sequence labeling task and adopt BiLSTM-CRF models. While BiLSTM-CRF models works relatively well on standard datasets it faces challenges in Chinese E-commerce slot filling due to more informative slot labels and richer expressions. In this paper, we propose a deep multi-task learning model with cascade and residual connections. Experimental results show that our framework not only achieves competitive performance with state-of-the-arts on a standard dataset, but also significantly outperforms strong baselines by a substantial gain of 14.6% on a Chinese E-commerce dataset.

Review #12258

  • Comments to Authors.

    This paper proposes a deep cascade BiLSTM model for Chinese E-commerce slot filling task, which splits up into two subtasks, NE tagging and segmentation, to promote the slot filling with cascade and residual connections. The authors also construct a slot filling dataset in Chinese E-commerce domain. Evaluation shows that the proposed method outperforms baselines. The way the slot filling task is divided into two subtasks is interesting. The authors have made the dataset available for research and they outline their experiments in detail. However, I have a few concerns about this paper.

    - It would be good to have brief discussion of whether the authors are using a Chinese word segmentation model to obtain the segment labels (BIO) for construction of ECSGA dataset.

    - I hope the authors can provide more details about the evaluation metric in Table 5. Is the PRF-score calculation based on the slot labels (only y^slot) or based on all of the following three labels (y^slot, y^ne, y^seg)?

    - Although the experimental comparison seems well done, unfortunately the evaluation does not include comparisons to the state-of-the-art approaches on ECSGA dataset (in Table 5). The baselines chosen in the paper are somehow weak. It's not surprising that author's approach beats the baseline methods, but the lack of comparison with state-of-the-art limits the paper's contribution. The experiments could be stronger by comparing with some other approaches, such as the systems in Table 4.

    Overall, although the new dataset may contribute to the community, the experimental comparison of the paper is quite marginal.

  • Metareview

    This paper proposes a deep cascade model for Chinese slot filling task, and constructs a slot filling dataset in E-commerce domain. The paper is well written.

    However, in their experiments, 1) it is not clear that whether the improvement comes from the model design or more parameters; 2) it is also not clear that whether the improvement comes from the shortcut connections or the multitask outputs; and 3) the evaluation does not include comparisons to the state-of-the-art approaches. The above problems limit the paper's contribution.

Review #20352

  • Comments to Authors.

    This paper proposes a multi-task RNN-based model that cascades different tasks in a strict order for slot filling. The main contributions are cascade and residual connections to enable high-level tasks taking the tagging results and hidden states of low-level tasks as additional input.

    In the experiments, the proposed cascade and residual connections seem to work well and help improve the performance. However, there are some issues to be addressed.

    • Parameter control: The proposed connections make the model require more parameters, so it is not clear whether the improvement comes from the model design or more parameters. It would be better to conduct the experiments using models with similar parameter sizes for fair comparison.
    • Novelty: The proposed model is very similar to hierarchical multu-task model. Two proposed connections may not contain enough originality.
    • Experiment: In the ATIS experiments, several baseline models achieve very similar performance as the proposed one. Hence, the comparison between these baselines and the proposed model is necessary to be included in the ECSGA experiments. Then the effectiveness of the proposed model can be justified.

    In sum, these issues should be addressed first in order to demonstrate the effectiveness of the model.

Review #25681

  • Comments to Authors.

    This paper proposes a more practical dataset for slot filling task and builds a deep multitask model with shortcut connections. The result is solid and significant.

    My main concern is the necessity of using the multitask framework for this problem, although the dataset has three level targets. I will argue this in the following statement.

    In Table 5, we can observe the performance gain heavily depends on the shortcut connections, rather than on the multitask outputs. The hierarchical multi-task only improves the F1 from 0.43 to 0.445, while with the help of the shortcuts, the performance raises up to 0.51. Although the shortcuts build on the multi-task labels, it would be reasonable to compare the basic BiLSTM-CRF with shortcuts with the DCMTL models to show the multitask gain. 

    I suggest the authors could build the model with deeper layers and try different kinds of shortcuts. It could be possible that a deeper model with a single target shows comparable performance with its multitask version.
